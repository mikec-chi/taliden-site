Trust & Governance

Governed by design. Auditable by default.

Enterprise agents cannot operate like uncontrolled scripts or ungoverned chatbots. Taliden gives organizations the control layer needed to run agents safely — with authority, policy, state, and human accountability built in.

01Trust principles

Human accountability

Separates what agents recommend, what systems automate, and what humans must decide.

Role-based authority

Agents operate within defined permissions, responsibilities, and approval boundaries.

State-aware execution

Actions validated against process state, business rules, and domain constraints.

Policy grounding

Recommendations checked against policy, operating procedures, and business rules.

Auditability

Records what the agent saw, recommended, did, which tools it used, who approved, and what changed.

Explainability

Explains decisions using business context, not just model-generated rationale.

02Work design

Human, agent, and system — each with a clear role.

Taliden separates what humans should decide, what agents can assist with, and what systems can automate. That boundary is what makes agentic work safe to scale.

Controlled integration

Every action leaves an evidence trail.

Agents access enterprise systems only through governed tools and approved actions. An agent may recommend a schedule change — but only an authorized manager can approve a high-risk override, and every step is recorded.
